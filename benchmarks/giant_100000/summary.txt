### Benchmark Highlights
* **Optimal Configuration**: The fastest inference time (30.37s) was achieved with 16 ranks and 1 intra-op thread, efficiently mapping exactly 1 process per physical core.
* **The Golden Rule**: Configurations where `ranks * intra = 16` consistently delivered the best performance (ranging from 30s to 34s).
* **Severe Oversubscription Penalty**: Exceeding the 16-core limit (`ranks * intra > 16`) caused catastrophic slowdowns (10x-50x slower) and timeouts due to thread contention.
* **Linear Memory Scaling**: Memory usage scales linearly, adding roughly 2.1 GB per rank, peaking at ~38.4 GB for 16 ranks.
* **Negligible Inter-op Impact**: Adjusting inter-op threads had zero meaningful effect on performance for this specific workload.
